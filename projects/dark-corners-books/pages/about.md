### The Story Behind the Stories

Dark Corners Books was born from a simple conviction: the best horror doesn't rely on gore or jump scares. It relies on *dread* — the slow, creeping realization that something is wrong, that the world doesn't work the way you thought it did, and that you are very, very small in the face of whatever is stirring in the dark.

We publish independent horror fiction across three primary veins:

- **Cosmic Horror** — The universe is vast, indifferent, and inhabited by things that don't know you exist and wouldn't care if they did. Lovecraftian in spirit, modern in execution.
- **Folk Horror** — The old stories were true. The land remembers. The rituals never really stopped. Drawing from Indian, Celtic, and Slavic folk traditions.
- **Psychological Horror** — The monster isn't under the bed. It's in your head. And it's been there a long time.


### The Author

Rajesh Radhakrishnan is a technology leader by day and a horror writer by night. Based in Kerala, India, he brings two decades of storytelling instinct to fiction — shaped by late nights reading Lovecraft, King, and Ligotti under a single lamp.

His day job: Senior Manager of Engineering at Cyncly, specializing in cloud architecture and AI systems.

His night job: Building worlds where the dark is darker than you think.

The intersection matters. An engineering mind understands structure. Horror requires structure — the careful architecture of dread, the pacing of revelation, the precise moment when the reader realizes they're holding their breath.

**Influences:** Thomas Ligotti, Laird Barron, Caitlín R. Kiernan, Stephen Graham Jones, T. Kingfisher, and the folk tales his grandmother told him in Kerala.
